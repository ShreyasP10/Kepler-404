// The Framework

The Pipeline

A deep learning framework designed for the Space Applications Centre / ISRO — from raw thermal capture to exportable colorized GeoTIFFs.

01

Data Ingest

Ingests low-resolution 200m single-channel Landsat 9 Band 10 (TIR) GeoTIFFs while preserving geospatial metadata, CRS, and affine transform.

02

Super-Resolution

Utilizes advanced generative AI (ESRGAN / SwinIR) to upscale structural details to 100m resolution, retaining thermal integrity and edge fidelity.

03

Colorization (RGB Translation)

Maps thermal signatures to realistic RGB representations using Pix2Pix / Diffusion models, enabling both human and AI interpretability.

04

Geospatial Export

Outputs fully colorized 100m GeoTIFFs with preserved CRS and transform, ready for overlay in QGIS or ArcGIS workflows.

// Questions

Frequently Asked

Understanding the TIR super-resolution and colorization technology.

It is a deep learning process that enhances the spatial resolution of low-resolution thermal satellite images (200m) to higher resolution (100m) by predicting missing structural details while preserving the thermal intensity values.

Generative AI models (GANs/Diffusion) learn the mapping between thermal signatures and visible RGB textures. The model uses a large dataset of paired Landsat 9 TIR and RGB images to accurately assign colors to thermal features.

This approach integrates physical laws of blackbody radiation into the loss function of the neural network. It ensures the AI doesn't just generate colors, but the generated RGB values are physically consistent with the measured thermal radiation.

Our framework incorporates strict structural loss (SSIM) and perceptual constraints to prevent hallucinations. Every generated feature is grounded in the input thermal data, ensuring no artificial structures are invented.
